Session 2

September 8th, 2024 (CE)
Flamecrest 1, 32 (3rd Age)

Now that the Stinkies no longer draw breath, the party gets a better look at their surroundings. The room they're in is full of broken musical instruments. Gerda retrieves her thrown filet knife from one of the creatures, and lights up the room to get a better look around. Several beautiful music boxes at the far end of the room become illuminated.

Penelope immediately goes to the trinkets, testing them out. Two are functional, but one of the boxes has a little figurine elf which looks to have once been able to dance, but no longer. Penelope puts the inoperative music box in her bag to fix later. Gerda reprimands Penelope for taking someone else's property, but the gnome holds her ground, citing adventurers' rights- there are no longer any living souls around with a better claim to the loot than the party.

Red examines one of the music boxes, noting its fairy with butterfly wings, indicative of the Summer court. He activates the device, listening to its tune. As it ends, the tiny fairy springs up with a slight crackle of static electricity. Red considers how strange it is to find so many items from the Feywild in this Yewdon manor, adding the music box to his bag.

Gerda examines the remaining music box, which has an elemental figure moving through a slow, controlled series of moves. Like her companions, Gerda stows the box in her bag. Aware of this seeming disparity to her earlier words to Penelope, Gerda comments that she wouldn't want to break up the set.

Friend picks at the harp, before riffling through some musical scrolls at the piano. They discard the scrolls with any musical notation, keeping the ones that were blank.

Now that combat has ended, Red wants to return to the room with the cavern roses. The others need no convincing to follow. Gerda and Red examine them together. The faint fragrance is reminiscent of honeysuckle; the purple and blue flowers- which have already bloomed once- are ready to bloom again.
These cavern roses are on the outer bounds of the flower patch, and have been growing unchecked for at least a year or two. Gerda suggests going through the nearby door, behind which there are likely more flowers. As she pushes it open, the air becomes noticeably colder.

In the next room is a banquet table that looks to be set up for a feast. Fine dishes and plush chairs surround the table, covered by many, many more cavern roses, and some big ones. The floor above the largest flower has collapsed downward, and Friend can see a faint blue pulsing light fading in and out through the hole to the room above.

Friend

Stars...

Red picks up a sample of one of the smaller flowers, breaking about half of the roots, but probably not enough to kill it. Gerda cuts a bit of the plant off, briefly staining her knife, before wiping it away.

The tips of the spiked vines sway slightly, despite there being no breeze. Friend wants to get to the "stars" by way of the plants, but Penelope suggests taking the stairs to get to the room above.

Upstairs, the party enters a greenhouse-like scene. The carpet is soaked and stained with rotting leaves. A stone enclosure surrounds a garden of three enormous trees and bunches of flowers. The leftmost tree is an oak, with bark that seems to glow with gold and amber. The center tree is blue, with metallic, glowing globes growing from its branches. On the right is a maple tree, which Red recognizes from his own Autumn court.

These three fey trees are young, perhaps 5 or 6 years old. Although they once had been planted and tended to, they seem to have been abandoned, not having been pruned or cared for recently.

Knowing little about these trees, Gerda goes to examine a desk, which holds an hourglass and some alchemical equipment. Most of the bottles are partially destroyed, their contents long since evaporated, but Gerda she finds two unbroken bottles- one contains a potion of comprehend languages, and the other is a potion of climbing.

Penelope moves on, opening the door that most directly leads to Friend's "star." The room is dusty, with desks full of ink bottles, ink stones, and packages wrapped in oil cloth. An open scroll can be seen, with writing in an elvish script. Some letters are folded into little paper tressym- cats with wings.

On the other side of the room is an altar that was once been arranged with flowers, ferns, and what might be memorial tablets. A symbol is visible, a spiral that blooms into leaves, denoting Kaza'ran. Gerda lights the candles, murmuring an apology to the deity of life for the altar's lack of care.

Red looks through the shelves and papers. The family seems to have been named Greysky. The documents are invitations, some half-written, others already folded into tressym. The letters are addressed to other Greysky families, inviting them to visit and see their success. Red surveys the room, so clearly set to entertain, and wonders aloud what happened and whether the visitors ever came.

Friend adds some of the ink stones to its collection. The bundled packets contain pale blue-grey leaves, which gave off a fine fragrance. Seeing no other immediate uses for the leaves, Friend proceeds to munch on the contents of one package. Gerda comments that it would be nice to have tea some evening. Friend obligingly hands the remaining packets over to the firbolg, who seems to know what to do with them, and has somewhat more refined intentions.

Penelope starts examining the north wall, reasoning that the area behind some ivy might be soft enough to punch through, and far more direct than taking the hallway which connected the rooms. She catapults the fern pot against the wall, successfully making a hole large enough to crawl through.

In the room beyond, a glowing, silvery box sits atop some splintered planks and boards, next to a hole in the floor. The box is a small, square chest, tipped over on its side. The outside shines with hints of mithral, while the inside is lined with lead (which Penelope explains has the ability to block detection magic). Its lid, which has a star on it, is swung open in the direction of the hole. Any contents the box may have once contained have long since fallen down to the floor below. Penelope wonders whether the cavern rose seeds were what it was meant to hold.

Friend looks through a stack of books. It leafs through one title in particular, Shameful Swordplay: How to Fake Being a Martial Archetype with any Style of Magic by Telvrin Melkar. The volume details the use of illusion and trickery to boost combat abilities, mimicking physical weapons with magic, and most importantly: how to win and NOT get caught when using the techniques described within. The book has been banned in the Irieaboreal Empire, to the east of Yewdon.

The next room, mostly overgrown with plants, is a huge bedroom, where the party decides to take a long rest.

Friend takes first watch.

Gerda and Penelope each dream of the barren plain and the twin trees.

Red trances.

He sees a necklace with a little sprig of red pine needles, something Delight always has with her. For the first time, instead of seeing a vision of the plain, he sees Delight's hand, fiddling with the necklace. She is in a spiral staircase going down, down, down. At first the footsteps are quick and energetic, but as the dream continues, Delight seems to be getting fatigued, as each step becomes more difficult.
Just before waking, Red sees a door. He cannot see what's inside, but a pale yellow-green light is shining under the door. For an instant, Red forgets Delight, feeling only the desire to get to the door and pass through it.

He awakens as Friend rouses him for his turn to take watch. It then takes up a spot on the floor, where it spends the remainder of the night, motionless.

Flamecrest 2, 32 (3rd Age)

Day comes again. Light pours in through the damaged ceiling.

Penelope, bright eyed from not having taken a watch, approaches the still motionless Friend, taking a few measurements and assessing the creature's general head shape. She makes some adjustments to the top hat she'd snagged from the closet the day before, modifying it to fit her towering Friend.
Upon completion, Friend chitters excitedly, striking poses and attempting to look regal in the hat they have long coveted.
The gnome isn't finished giving gifts, though. She approaches Gerda, offering her a set of dark vision goggles. They are similar to the ones she herself wears, but they have clearly been designed to fit the firbolg's more homey, utilitarian style, rather than Penelope's flair for the flamboyant.
Throughout all this, the artificer is noticeably less encumbered by the many backpacks and bags she'd previously been carrying. In explanation, she shows off her new fanny pack of holding, and offers to carry things for her companions as well.

The party discusses their next course of action. Penelope wants to continue looking around before departing toward the twin trees. It wouldn't be very heroic to leave these problematic roses to grow and spread.

Using his smithing tools, Red examines the mithral box they'd found the previous day. Constellations decorate the exterior, with each star made up of a draconic character. The exquisite craftsmanship combined with the draconic script indicate that this box was constructed by a metallic dragon, or someone who studied under one.

At Penelope's request, Friend tosses the gnome across the hole in the floor of the room. Penelope lands on the far side and opens a door to find a vine-filled hallway. She follows it around, sneaking to the far side of the party, purposefully startling her companions.

Gerda, having only just been spooked by Penelope, feels a light tickle on her neck. She is startled to find a tawny owl feather to be the source, but looking up to the various holes in the ceiling, can see nothing to indicate its origin. Disconcerted, she pockets the feather.

The firbolg suggests dangling Penelope down through the hole in the floor, to the maw of the enormous flower below, where whatever was in the silver box must have fallen. Although this proposal was made somewhat wryly, the gnome's approval is sincere.

Penelope

"I'm ready to be a hero!"

They fashion a climbing harness and fling the free line over a beam that still seems secure. A second line of rope is looped and knotted around Gerda, who will act as an anchor, keeping Penelope from falling too far. Friend is the one lowering the rope.

"Don't think of squids," Penelope repeats to herself as she slowly descends.

Struck with inspiration, Penelope requests that she be pulled up again briefly. She tinkers and fiddles for a few minutes, fashioning a Grabby Claw to extend her reach by 5 feet.

She gets lowered once more to the floral maw, new device in hand. As she pokes around, the flower begins to close around the claw, and Penelope narrowly withdraws her tool before the petals surround it completely.

She notices that the cool mist they've been seeing in the manor seems to be emitted from the maw.
Red observes that the little tips of the vines start moving a bit more, as though it is beginning to wake.

Gerda

"I'm willing to risk Penelope one more time."

For the third time, Penelope is pulleyed down until she can reach around in the plant once more. The claw clinks against something hard and she withdraws her tool with the object in its clasp.

Friend pulls Penelope up, and as she rises, so does the cold and mist.

Penelope produces the lead-lined box from her pack and drops the prize inside, where it can finally be seen by the party.

The item is a metallic, blue-grey pearl the size of a walnut. Friend feels something akin to nausea from the item's proximity, and Red is likewise unsettled. He names the pearl as cold iron, a material that is deadly to fey.
