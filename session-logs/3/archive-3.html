Session 3

September 15th, 2024 (CE)
Flamecrest 2, 32 (3rd Age)

After retrieving the lump of cold iron from the cavern rose, the party takes a break, munching some rations as they discuss their next moves. Shut in the lead-lined box, it was no longer a danger to anyone. But, as Penelope points out, while they may have removed the cold iron that was empowering the cavern roses, the plants themselves remain a threat to the local ecosystem. Gerda, not necessarily in disagreement, asks Penelope about her reasons for taking on the responsibility. The gnome reiterates that the party's status as "heroes" demands that they take action. Gerda responds that she'll keep a lookout for some herbicide.

At that, they begin to tour the remaining rooms in the manor. They start in what turns out to be quite a fancy bathroom, with lotions and glass pitchers on its adorned counters.

Next they look through some bedrooms. The toys, dollhouse, and size of the furniture suggest that the occupants had been children. There is a small, partially-packed suitcase on the floor. The bottom layer is mostly toys, with some clothes sensibly piled on top, as though some adult had come along to pack essentials after the child had only packed playthings. However, the fact that the suitcase remains indicates that although it was packed, there wasn't time in the end for its owner to grab it.

By way of a common area with some furniture in the hall, the party returns to the arboretum. Checking the alchemical table again for possible herbicide ingredients, Penelope finds a pouch of what looks like ash, labeled 'Caustic.' They determine the powder to be ashes from an ironscale hydra, a creature with acidic blood. This substance is used to eradicate parasitic infections, typically used underground.

The party debates whether to keep the powder in dry form, or perhaps mixed with water. Penelope suggests a catapult, though Gerda recommends pouring the mix through the hole in the ceiling above the plants.

Gerda and Red combine their "plantology" knowledge, hatching a plan to attack the plants at the center of the largest mass.

They go upstairs.

The room across from the music room is a library. The books are waterlogged, but largely in good condition. Drop clothes cover the majority of the furniture in the room.

A topographical map of a subterranean area can be seen on the center table. The map appears to be of a drow community (a subterranean race of elves with extraordinary dark vision, but considerable light sensitivity), called Ungethal.

Friend has seen more detailed maps of these areas, showing collapsed and damaged passages around some of the enormous caverns that collapsed in the Spellplague. Friend works on making a replication of this three-dimensional map on some parchment. Gerda takes careful note of the map as well, making a remarkably good copy. Friend watches with curiosity and approval.

One table has its drop cloth pulled aside. Based on the relative amounts of dust collected, Gerda estimates that someone has come through the room in the past year. On the table sits another ornate box that matches the craftsmanship of the mithral box the party found the previous day. This one has many bands of metal (gold, silver, bronze, copper, and brass) weaving around its faces. The box is closed and latched, but unlocked.

To one side of the box is another music box. To the other side is an empty place where there something else looks to have been placed.

Penelope excitedly informs the party that the different bands of metal correspond with the metallic dragon types, another connection to dragons which the gnome sees as more than coincidence. Red examines this new box, seeing no writing, just the intricate inlay and weaving of the metals. Gerda flips the latch and opens the metal box, which is empty.

The music box, when shut, has the appearance of an egg. When opened, a little copper wyrmling constructed of intricate copper gears begins to rise, rearing its little head into a roar. As the only party member without a music box, Friend becomes the new owner of this one.

Moving on to the shelves, the party members look through the collection of books:

Red:

Example

Paradoxomicon: Collected works of a plane-shifter wizard who has dedicated his life to finding loopholes in magic and testing them in parallel planes of existence, collapsing each one of them in doing so.

Gerda:

Example

The Convergent Truth': The Convergent Truth is a detailed explanation about why the plane you are in right now is a demi-plane, an exact copy of the material plane, used to lure something called The Devourer, the Unmaker, away from the true material plane.

Penelope:

Example

Loves Return: This book is pale green with black lettering and an embossed cartoonish skull in the center of the cover. Inside this book is a tale in the style of a children's story about losing a loved one and how to bring them back from beyond the grave. Disturbingly the book gives a fully functional process of ritually summoning an undead with such ease.

Friend:

Example

The Writings Of Theon Ganderson': A small black journal kept by a man who claimed to have spotted a 'thing' living under his farm house. This 'thing' turned out to be a small abomination being that began eating Theon's livestock. The journal ends abruptly during a passage where Theon is describing hearing something underneath his floorboards.

The remaining unexplored area of the house is an enormous ballroom. Remnants of a smashed chandelier coat the floor. Its candles have long scratches that match the shape of the Stinkies' teeth. At the center of the floor is a fountain that has long since dried up. Archways and trellises adorn the outer edges of the room with large metal urns on display.

The party sifts through the rubble. Gerda finds a few candles in good condition. Friend collects a few glass bottles that are sealed with wax. There are finely painted labels on the outside: one depicts a three-headed dog, with its heads breathing fire, breathing frost, and with blood dripping from its muzzle, respectively. Another bottle has a nice countryside painting. Uninterested in alcohol as a consumable, Friend keeps the bottles anyway, perhaps to try as a cleaning solution.

Red examines the urns. They contain ashes, likely the ancestors of the Greysky family, with names inlaid in a beautiful script. The elvish surname Ianna, which translates to "sky of stone" or "grey sky" in Common, accompanies the following names listed:

Recite

Sundamar
Corym
Syvis

The party returns to the room above the cavern roses. Penelope puts on all of her protective gear and portions out half of the caustic powder, allowing herself room for a second attempt. Gerda offers to help as well. Penelope agrees, giving her some gloves and instructing her to don her goggles. She ejects the rest of the party from the room.

Penelope

"Hazmat rules."

Using her grabby claw to extend her reach over the hole in the floor above the plants, Penelope drops the little packet of powder.
Anticlimactically, there is no immediate change as the dust cloud spreads in the flower room. The party decides that the job's probably done, but they'll come back in a week to make sure the plants are all dead.

It's now just coming on to noon. Gerda remarks that if they leave now, they should be able to get to the trees on the barren plain by nightfall. Penelope invites Friend to accompany them to the trees, who eagerly agrees, making a stop by their well to grab the essentials, including Daisy.

The party leaves the manor behind. The further away they get, the harder the ground gets. The grass fades, and what little there is left is yellow and dried. The plant life variety diminishes to some broadleaf plants and grass strands.

Penelope, who is unaccustomed to such corporeal-based transportation, spends about an hour trotting to keep up, before requesting a piggyback ride from Friend, who wordlessly agrees.

As they travel, Red's cloak fades from its vibrant red and orange to the dull green, pale yellow that surrounds them. Friend's carapace likewise camouflages to match the environmental hues. Penelope is talking non-stop to Friend, explaining the minutiae of the artificer class. Friend listens politely, retaining nothing.

Finally, they arrive at the trees. Gerda is the only one who has been here before, back when it used to be a forest. But the wood has gone brittle and dry. The plants have patches where the veins of the leaves grow black. Sap has dried in corrupted, grey-colored clumps.

A strange combination of noises can be heard coming from nearby: the growling of a lion, the snapping of a crocodile jaw, followed by, "Fuck off, asshole." A clang of metal rings out, and the sound of pain.

From far off, only two trees can be seen but as the party approaches, in the space between the two huge trees they see what appears to be the sheared off trunk of a third tree. Gerda points to some odd shapes atop the stump. A creature battling what seem to be several giant, floating eyes with tentacles. In the center is a tall, wingless crow person swinging a glaive and making a noise that sounds like a dragon's roar.

??

"Bahamat's ballsack!"

The kenku continues making various intimidating animal noises and glaives one of the creatures. The eye beast deflates and expels a cloud of gas, which floats toward the kenku. "That was stupid," he coughs, before losing consciousness.

Penelope, still on Friend's back, urges her ride forward toward the fallen stranger. The gnome pulls her bandana up and her mask down. Friend complies with no hesitation, leaving Red and Gerda behind. The firbolg carefully watches for the cloud to dissipate, tying a handkerchief around her mouth, before approaching the scene with Red.
From this distance, the Eladrin believes the floating eye creatures to be beholders, and calls out a warning to his companions about their terrifying abilities.

The area is hazy and cloudy with what seems to be a pollen or dust.

Red, despite his distance, makes the first attack with his longbow. His arrow hits the nearest creature, which bursts into another cloud of gas, though Red is far enough away to be unaffected.

Penelope, still atop Friend's shoulders, fires at another with her crossbow, which likewise poofs.

Friend moves in a stabs the remaining creature, killing it. The gas seeps out, and Friend is not able to escape it in time. They feel sluggish, as though poisoned, and sit to recover, now that combat has ended. Penelope, fortunately, was not affected by the gas.

The party approaches the motionless kenku. As Penelope stabilizes him, she notices many layers of the pollen dust on his cloak, indicating that he has probably been in the area considerably longer. Now conscious, he opens his eyes and looks around. "It's this much my favorite," he says in a child's voice, indicating a small space between his talons.
Penelope asks if he is hurt. "Unclear," comes the response.

The kenku make some clicking noises, followed by, "Hi, I'm Click!"

Penelope introduces herself, Gerda, and then Friend. The insectoid makes a weak gesture of greeting. Next she presents the elf as Red, who corrects her.

Elwing

"My name is Elwing."

Gerda asks Click if he knows what happened to the trees. "I could share, if you want," comes the response, to which the party all agrees. Click pulls out a slate and chalk and begins writing for a while.

Meanwhile, Penelope asks after Friend, who sinks lower. She cures its wounds with her hammer. Friend's carapace undulates as it heals, but ass the colors shift, the tips of Friend's hands no longer change, they just remain black.

Gerda and Elwing begin to notice draconic letters that are carved into the wood of the tree trunk they are all atop:

Recite

"In the silver light of Krexis Tlaxxar, amongst his wyrm-touched wonders you tread. Through curiosity's veins, rooted deep, let your journey sprout. Fear not, mortal seedling, for you are sheltered in the timbered heart of discovery."

Penelope points out the connection to Bahamat, whom Click had invoked earlier, with the draconic letters. Bahamat is the father of righteousness, a great dragon. Many believe that it was out of Bahamat's imagination that the first dragons were created.

Click

"I am Click. From The Shadowfel."

Click's slate details how he had nightmares about the eggs of his family being smashed. When he begged the Raven Queen for answers, he was told to seek the two trees that remain, that have outlasted the corruption that poisoned the forest. He set off and found a path toward Yewdon, and is searching for the next clue. He mentions having gotten his "feathers plucked," and that he is weakened by the effects of poison.

Penelope asks Gerda whether she knows anything about the pollen. Gerda and Elwing examine it, and become convinced that it isn't actually plant material, but more like dense mushroom spores.
Looking at how withered and dried the plants are all around, from the way the pollen reacts, it's almost as though it's aware. It reminds Gerda of the way demons begin to affect their environments to better suit them. This demon phage, a virus, is an infection that will continue to grow and change. if it progresses too far, it will first kill Friend, and then raise them as a demon.

Elwing casts a spell to detect poison and disease. Basically everything around him is glowing. The plants, the creatures scurrying in the dirt; everything but the tree. The hue of disease is likewise emanating from Friend and Click.

The party looks around for some refuge from the poison. Gerda wants to leave, but Elwing suggests that there there might be safety within the tree itself. Everyone looks around for some kind of entrance.
A set of runes found in the roots of the tree surround a staircase and door leading into the tree. The path seems to be free from the phage.

Gerda takes her potion of comprehension to read the celestial writing around the door:

Recite

It is with great pleasure that I welcome you to my sanctum, a veritable trove of the strange and marvelous. Gaze upon the iridescent blooms of the Celestial Lotus, whose petals shimmer with the light of a thousand stars. Marvel at the Void-Forged Chalice, a vessel that seems to drink in the very shadows that surround it. And if you dare, step closer to the Obsidian Shard, a fragment of a fallen comet that whispers secrets of the cosmos.

The party gets the impression of a museum attraction as they enter the trunk steps leading down, inviting Click to join them. "It's THIS much my favorite," he exclaims happily.

They enter the tree, quickly closing the door behind them. The air inside is fresher, and the floor of the hollow tree is intricately carved. They descend the staircase and enter a room with a pedestal in the center. A large piece of blank parchment and a silver feather float above the pedestal. Across the room is an archway in the wall.
A voice begins to speak in many languages.

Callout

"What do you seek? Who are you?"

Penelope says, "Hiya, I'm Penelope!" The quill writes out her name.

"I'm Gerda, and I would love a resolution to this blasted dream," says the firbolg.

Elwing gives his name and reiterates Gerda's desire for answers.

"Click. Hero."

Penelope, realizing suddenly that she'd neglected to state her goal, interjects that she wants to stop the demons.

Friend initially introduces itself telepathically, but there was no indication from the quill and parchment that the message was received. For the first time, in makes a series of audible chittering noises, which seem to be successfully communicated, as a series of alien markings are written on the parchment. "Fix bad feeling," Friend concluded, indicating its discolored fingertips.

After each member speaks their name and purpose, the archway turns into a doorway, with filigree filling the previously unadorned space.


Gerda asks Penelope what she knows about silver dragons, making a connection to the silver feather quill.

"They like helping people, and hearing about how much they help people," Penelope replied.

Gold pattern of the floor looks like it's been scratched in the floor by a giant claw, then filled in with a gold dust sealed into the floor, giving the room a constant divination effect. This divination array might help detect magical effects and items within the room.

In the next room, a larger space, is a large table covered with bits of paper, parchment, scrolls, a bottle with a cork in it with a letter curled up within, maps, drawings, portraits, notes. Each piece is writtenby a different hand, in many different languages. Each seems to be a firsthand account of someone who has passed through the room.

Friend picks up a map from the table, and images from the different notations illuminate in the air as though the group can see the memories as glimpses from the perspective of the writer.

Gerda looks about for writing implements. She writes a brief synopsis of what took place at the manor house, holding up the page in the same way that Friend had, and sees visual depictions of the description she'd just written.

Experimentally, Penelope holds up her journal to the first page she'd already written, but nothing happens.

Gerda picks up the paper she'd written on, folding it up and putting it in her pocket, distressed at the idea of leaving her memories behind.

Friend takes the map it picked up to examine, rolls it up and slides it in with its other maps, before examining the room itself. They note illusion and evocation magic around the table.

Penelope scans a paper that describes the first seven trees of Rakāuvi. She learns that acorns from the first Oak possess "true resurrection magic." Then, Penelope sketches out a copy of the schematic for her grabby claw, leaving it on the table.

Friend draws crude drawings of its companions, including Click, offering it to the table.

[Click draws with chalk for a while] image depicting his not yet hatched chick he wants to meet and some simple melodies that his community uses to send messages

Elwing writes up a paper on the properties of the cavern roses and offers it to the table.
Images of his home, the Autumn forest, fill the air with huge trees and a jagged rift where the cavern roses grow.

When the last offering is given, a door appears, leading down a hallway.

The next room reaches up higher than any room so far, with the ceiling out of sight. There are 4 alcoves about the room, three of them with shining, gleaming statues. In the center of the room is a fountain with 4 colors of dragon heads spitting water. A silver dragon sits above them all, facing the empty alcove, not spitting water. In the fountain, charms and coins are in the water.

The statues in the alcoves are each about 10 feet tall.
The first is an older dwarvish man with a thick beard. He holds a closed book and a crystal ball, within which there is a stylized eye.
The next is a tall elvish woman. An ivy crown grows out of her hair, which is tied back. Around her neck is a necklace with a unicorn pendant. She carries a bow, which is drawn.
The third statue is a tiefling, holding an axe in each hand, crossed over their chest. Only stumps remain where magnificent horns once grew, apparently having been chopped off. One eye looks to be made of ruby- the other has been pried out.

Gerda notices that each of the three statues has an element that points to the empty alcove; the gaze of the eye, the direction of the drawn bow, the face of the tiefling. She walks into the alcove. Fuchsia light begins to glow; a few moments pass. Then Gerda disappears.

Alarmed, Elwing begins to tell his remaining companions that he wants to keep the party together, but he could scarcely open his mouth to speak before Penelope, followed by Click, enter the alcove as well. They strike a pose and vanish. Friend doesn't want to be left behind. Elwing takes up the rear.
